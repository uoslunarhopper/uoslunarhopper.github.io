  • 400N Hybrid Rocket Motor

    Built by the Phase III team, the 400N hybrid rocket motor is the powerhouse of the Lunar Hopper. It is a hybrid motor, meaning it uses a solid fuel and liquid oxidiser.

    The solid fuel grain is formed into a hollow cylinder in the main body of the rocket. The liquid oxidiser is high-test peroxide (HTP).

    Together the two are able to produce 400N of thrust for the Hopper, allowing it to fulfil its mission.

  • Attitude Control System

    Keeping the Hopper steady during flight is one of the key challenges of the project. The attitude control system (ACS) was started by Phase VI, and is a reactionary control system.

    An accelerometer and gyroscopes determine the Hopper's attitude, and if it begins to tilt off-course, the ACS system will fire one of the four thrusters at the top of the Hopper, pushing the craft in the oposite direction.

    The system uses inert nitrogen gas as the propellant for the trusters.

  • OxOxidiser Tanks

    These high-pressure tanks hold the oxidiser, HTP, used by the rocket motor at a safe pressure.

  • N2Nitrogen Gas Tanks

    The nitrogen in these tanks is used by the ACS system to keep the craft stable, and also to pressurise the oxidiser feed system for the rocket motor.
